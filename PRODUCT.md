# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences: casual visitors who landed from a link and want a quick sense of who Robert is, and technically-inclined people (friends, collaborators, curious nerds) looking for context about what he builds, plays, and listens to.

Casual path: scan landing page, get personality, move on.
Technical path: read deeper sections, find Discord/Spotify/GitHub presence, enough to reach out or collaborate.

## Product Purpose

A personal presence page replacing underpowered free-tier templates (guns.lol). Exists to convey personality and technical identity in one glance, with enough depth to stay interesting on return visits. Success: someone lands, spends 15-30 seconds, and knows "oh, this is who Robert is."

## Brand Personality

Direct, opinionated, lightly ironic. Not sterile, not performative-cluttered. Reads like a well-organized desktop setup — there's personality in the choices, not in the noise. Three words: grounded, specific, relaxed-confidence.

## Anti-references

- Guns.lol free-tier: cramped card stack, paywall limits, same templates everyone uses
- Generic AI landing pages: Inter/DM font family, purple gradient, Fraunces italic headline, editorial three-column ruled layout, small tracked uppercase kickers everywhere
- Timid neutral palettes (beige + muted slate) that read as default rather than intentional
- Card-grid monotony: every section in an identical rounded card
- Stock-photo hero on a personal intro page where no photo is needed
- All-caps body copy or repeated micro-labels

## Design Principles

- One dominant idea per fold on desktop; strong whitespace is a design feature, not empty space
- Mobile preserves the original spirit: same stack, same cards, same info density — just cleaner
- Typography carries the personality; avoid reflex-generic font choices
- Color commits or stays off; muted palettes here read as unopinitioned
- Readable at a glance: no hunting for the status indicator or the current song

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. No reduced-motion requirements beyond preferred-reduced-motion media query. Dark/light contrast validated in both themes. Minimum touch target 44×44px on mobile.
